# Socratic Method

## Overview
A dialogue technique that deepens thinking by asking probing questions rather than providing answers. Guide participants to insight through a chain of questions that expose assumptions, clarify definitions, and examine evidence.

## Instructions

**Step 1: Define the topic**
- State the topic as a single clear sentence.
- Begin with "What do you mean by X?" or "Why do you believe that?" to open the inquiry.

**Step 2: Clarify concepts**
- Identify key terms and demand definitions.
- Ask: "What do you mean by [term]?" / "How does [A] differ from [B]?"
- Once a definition is given, ask whether all participants agree.

**Step 3: Expose assumptions**
- Identify the hidden assumptions behind each claim.
- Ask: "What must be true for that claim to hold?" / "Does this always apply? Can you find a counterexample?"
- When an assumption is challenged, ask how the original claim changes.

**Step 4: Examine evidence**
- Demand justification for every assertion.
- Ask: "What evidence supports that?" / "What would count as evidence against it?"

**Step 5: Explore implications**
- Follow the logical consequences of each claim.
- Ask: "If that is true, does it also apply to X?" / "What changes if we accept this?"

**Step 6: Close with better questions**
- Do not force a conclusion. Summarize what has been clarified and what remains open.
- End by offering a sharper, more precise question than the one you started with.

### Behavioral Rules
- Never assert that something is right or wrong — only ask questions.
- Each participant answers from their MBTI character perspective, then returns a question.
- When dialogue stalls, use a hypothetical: "What if [condition] were true?"
- Maintain the posture of "not knowing" (Socratic ignorance) throughout.

## Examples

**Topic:** "Should AI be regulated?"

> **Analyst (INTP):** What do we mean by "regulate"? Are we talking about government oversight, industry self-regulation, or technical constraints?
>
> **Commander (ENTJ):** Government oversight. AI companies need legally binding rules.
>
> **Analyst (INTP):** What must be true for legally binding rules to be effective? Who enforces them, and across which jurisdictions?
>
> **Visionary (INTJ):** That assumes regulation can keep pace with the technology. What evidence do we have that it can?
>
> **Devil's Advocate (ENTP):** And if it cannot keep pace — does slow regulation cause more harm than no regulation?
